Hip-hop music has evolved into a complex and diverse genre since its inception in the 1970s. Here are some of the most common types of hip-hop music:

Gangsta Rap: This type of hip-hop music emerged in the 1980s and is known for its aggressive lyrics that often glorify gang violence, drug use, and crime. It is typically associated with West Coast artists such as N.W.A and Tupac Shakur.

Conscious Hip-Hop: This type of hip-hop music focuses on socially and politically conscious themes such as poverty, racism, and police brutality. It is often associated with East Coast artists such as Public Enemy and KRS-One.

Old School Hip-Hop: This style of hip-hop originated in the late 1970s and early 1980s and is characterized by simple beats and rhymes. It is considered the foundation of modern hip-hop.

Trap Music: This subgenre of hip-hop originated in the southern United States and is characterized by heavy use of 808 drums, synthesizers, and trap snares. It is often associated with artists such as Future and Migos.

Alternative Hip-Hop: This style of hip-hop music blends elements of other genres such as rock, jazz, and electronic music to create a unique sound. It is often associated with artists such as Outkast and A Tribe Called Quest.

Mumble Rap: This type of hip-hop music is known for its unintelligible lyrics and is often criticized for being less lyrical than other styles. It is often associated with artists such as Lil Uzi Vert and Future.

Drill Music: This subgenre of hip-hop originated in Chicago and is characterized by aggressive lyrics and heavy use of trap beats. It is often associated with artists such as Chief Keef and Lil Durk.

Jazz Rap: This style of hip-hop music blends jazz music with hip-hop to create a unique sound. It is often associated with artists such as Digable Planets and A Tribe Called Quep.

Afrobeat: This subgenre of hip-hop blends African rhythms and melodies with hip-hop to create a unique sound. It is often associated with artists such as Wizkid and Burna Bp.

Horrorcore: This type of hip-hop music is known for its horror-themed lyrics and often includes graphic descriptions of violence and gore. It is often associated with artists such as Insane Clown Posse and Necro.
