Classical music is a genre of music that has a rich and diverse history spanning several centuries. It is typically characterized by its use of orchestral instruments, complex harmonies, and sophisticated musical structures. Here are some of the different types of classical music:

Baroque: This type of classical music originated in the 17th century and is known for its ornate, intricate melodies and complex contrapuntal textures.Famous Baroque composers include Johann Sebastian Bach and George Frideric Handel.

Classical: This period of music emerged in the mid-18th century and is characterized by its simpler, more elegant melodies and clearer, more balanced forms. The most famous classical composer is Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart.

Romantic: The Romantic period of classical music spanned the 19th century and is known for its emotional intensity, lush harmonies, and expansive musical forms. Famous composers from this period include Ludwig van Beethoven, Frederic Chopin, and Franz Liszt.

Impressionist: This type of classical music emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries and is characterized by its use of harmonic color and atmospheric textures. Famous Impressionist composers include Claude Debussy and Maurice Ravel.

Modern: This is a broad term used to describe classical music written from the early 20th century to the present day. Modern classical music often employs complex harmonies, unconventional tonal structures, and experimental techniques. Famous modern composers include Igor Stravinsky and Arnold Schoenberg.

Contemporary: This type of classical music is written by composers living and working today. It encompasses a wide range of styles and approaches, from the avant-garde to the neo-Romantic. Famous contemporary composers include John Adams and Philip Glass.
